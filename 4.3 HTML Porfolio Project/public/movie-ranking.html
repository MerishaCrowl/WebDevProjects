Merisha's Media Rankings

My top 10 movies of all-time.

1. Firestarter

Firestarter is a 1984 American science fiction thriller horror film based on Stephen King's 1980 novel of the same name.[4] The plot concerns a girl who develops pyrokinesis and the secret government agency known as The Shop which seeks to control her. The film was directed by Mark L. Lester, and stars David Keith, Drew Barrymore, Martin Sheen and George C. Scott.

This was my favorite movie of all-time, I watched it on repeat as a child and still love it.

Drew Barrymore is fierce.

2. Demolition Man

Demolition Man (1993) is a sci-fi action film starring Sylvester Stallone and Wesley Snipes. After being cryogenically frozen in 1996, cop John Spartan and criminal Simon Phoenix wake in 2032. Phoenix wreaks havoc in the futuristic city of San Angeles, and Spartan, with officer Lenina Huxley, must stop him. They defeat Phoenix, destroy the cryo-prison, and Spartan urges the city to balance order with freedom.

Hilarious movie from when the world was good.

3. The Book of Eli

Directed by the Hughes brothers and starring Denzel Washington The Book of Eli (2010) follows Eli, a nomad in a post-apocalyptic U.S., who carries the last surviving Bible to a safe sanctuary on the West Coast. Facing gangs and the warlord Carnegie, Eli is aided by Solara and ultimately reaches Alcatraz Island, where he dictates the Bible from memory before dying, ensuring its preservation and inspiring Solara to continue his mission.

Really great movie I watched with my favorite people in the world.

4. The Shack

In "The Shack" (2017), Mack Phillips (played by Sam Worthington) experiences a profound family tragedy when his youngest daughter, Missy, is kidnapped during a family vacation. Following her presumed death, Mack receives a mysterious letter inviting him to an abandoned shack in the Oregon wilderness, which leads him to confront his crisis of faith and inner turmoil. The film explores themes of loss, forgiveness, and the nature of God as Mack embarks on a transformative journey.

This movie changed me.

5. Dirty Dancing

Dirty Dancing is a 1987 American romantic drama dance film written by Eleanor Bergstein, produced by Linda Gottlieb, and directed by Emile Ardolino. Starring Patrick Swayze and Jennifer Grey, it tells the story of Frances "Baby" Houseman (Grey), a young woman who falls in love with dance instructor Johnny Castle (Swayze) at a vacation resort in the 1963 Borscht Belt.

Greatest love story ever told. Nobody puts Baby in a corner.

6. Don't Tell Mom The Babysitter's Dead

A 1991 American coming-of-age black comedy film directed by Stephen Herek, written by Neil Landau and Tara Ison, and starring Christina Applegate, Joanna Cassidy, Keith Coogan, John Getz, and Josh Charles. The plot focuses on a 17-year-old girl who assumes the role as head of the house when the elderly babysitter whom her mother had hired to watch over her kids while she is in Australia suddenly dies.

I'm right on top of that Rose.

7. Neverending Story

The Neverending Story (German: Die unendliche Geschichte) is a fantasy novel by German writer Michael Ende, published in 1979. The story follows a young boy named Bastian who discovers a magical book that transports him to the world of Fantastica, where he must help save it from destruction. The novel was adapted into a popular 1984 film directed by Wolfgang Petersen, which has since become a classic.

Never give up and good luck will find you.

8. Life of Pi

Life of Pi is a 2012 adventure-drama film directed and produced by Ang Lee and written by David Magee. Based on Yann Martel's 2001 novel, it stars Suraj Sharma in his film debut, Irrfan Khan, Tabu, Rafe Spall, Gérard Depardieu and Adil Hussain in lead roles. The storyline revolves around Pi Patel, a 16-year old Indian, and a Bengal tiger named Richard Parker, who are both stranded on a lifeboat for 227 days after a shipwreck.

Never underestimate a man's ability to underestimate a woman.

9. Wild Hearts Can't Be Broken

In 1932, Sonora Webster escapes her abusive aunt to pursue her dream of becoming a diving horse girl. She joins Doc Carver’s traveling show, bonds with a wild horse named Lightning, and eventually becomes the star performer, while falling in love with Doc’s son, Al. After Doc’s death, Sonora continues performing in Atlantic City until a tragic accident leaves her permanently blind. Refusing to give up, she secretly dives again, proving her courage and resilience. She goes on to perform for over a decade without audiences knowing of her blindness, building both a career and a happy marriage with Al.

Manure just happens to be my speciality.

10. Love & Basketball

Love & Basketball is a 2000 American romantic sports drama film written and directed by Gina Prince-Bythewood in her feature film directorial debut. The film is produced by Spike Lee and Sam Kit and stars Sanaa Lathan and Omar Epps. It tells the story of Quincy McCall (Epps) and Monica Wright (Lathan), two next-door neighbors in Los Angeles, who are pursuing their respective basketball careers before eventually falling for each other.

I've been in love with you since I was 11 and the shit won't go away.

My top 10 television shows of all-time.

1. Gray's Anatomy

Grey’s Anatomy follows Meredith Grey, daughter of renowned surgeon Ellis Grey, as she begins her surgical residency at Seattle Grace Hospital. Alongside fellow interns Cristina Yang, Izzie Stevens, Alex Karev, and George O’Malley, Meredith navigates the challenges of medicine, demanding schedules, and personal relationships. Guided by resident Miranda Bailey and attendings Derek Shepherd, Preston Burke, and Richard Webber, the interns face both professional and romantic entanglements. Over the first six seasons, Burke, O’Malley, and Stevens depart, shaping the evolving dynamics of the hospital.

“Don’t let fear keep you quiet. You have a voice, so use it. Speak up. Raise your hands. Shout your answers. Make yourself heard. Whatever it takes, just find your voice and when you do, fill the damn silence.” — Meredith Grey

2. Scandal

"Scandal" aired on ABC from April 5, 2012, to April 19, 2018, spanning seven seasons and 124 episodes. The series follows Olivia Pope, a former White House Communications Director who starts her own crisis management firm, Olivia Pope & Associates (OPA). Olivia and her team, often referred to as "Gladiators," handle high-profile scandals while dealing with their own personal issues and secrets. The show intricately weaves political intrigue, romance, and moral dilemmas, particularly focusing on Olivia's complicated relationship with President Fitzgerald Grant (played by Tony Goldwyn).

I know you.

3. Homeland

Homeland (stylized as HOMƎLAND) is an American espionage thriller television series developed by Howard Gordon and Alex Gansa. Based on the Israeli series Prisoners of War (Hebrew: חטופים, romanized: Hatufim, literally "Abductees"), it was created by Gideon Raff,[1][2] who also serves as an executive producer on Homeland. The series stars Claire Danes as Carrie Mathison, a CIA officer with bipolar disorder, convinced that decorated Marine Corps scout sniper Nicholas Brody (Damian Lewis) was "turned" by al-Qaeda and poses a threat to the United States. The series storyline grows from that premise, together with Mathison's ongoing covert work.

The theme song to this show puts me to sleep, and I can rewatch this show a million times from beginning to end and never get bored of it.

4. Fringe

"Fringe" follows the story of FBI agent Olivia Dunham (played by Anna Torv), who teams up with Walter Bishop (John Noble), a brilliant but institutionalized scientist, and his son Peter Bishop (Joshua Jackson). Together, they form the Fringe Division, a special task force that investigates bizarre and often gruesome occurrences linked to fringe science, including parallel universes and advanced biotechnology. The series begins with standalone cases but evolves into a complex narrative involving alternate realities and overarching conspiracies.

I binge watched this series multiple times. Great show.

5. Shameless

Shameless is an American comedy drama television series developed by John Wells that aired on Showtime from January 9, 2011, to April 11, 2021. It is an adaptation of Paul Abbott's British series of the same name and features an ensemble cast led by William H. Macy and Emmy Rossum. The series is set in the South Side of Chicago, Illinois.

“Just because we were born here, doesn’t mean that we end up here.” – Mandy Milkovich

6. Person of Interest

Person of Interest is a science fiction crime drama that aired on CBS from 2011 to 2016, spanning five seasons and 103 episodes. Created by Jonathan Nolan and produced by a team including J.J. Abrams, the show follows Harold Finch, a reclusive billionaire and computer genius, who builds "the Machine"—a surveillance system designed to predict terrorist threats. Instead, Finch uses it to prevent everyday crimes by partnering with John Reese, a former CIA operative. The series explores complex ethical themes such as privacy, morality, and the consequences of using artificial intelligence to make life-and-death decisions.

“I traveled the world looking for bad guys, but there were plenty of you right here all along.”

7. Chicago Med

Chicago Med is an American medical drama series broadcast by NBC and created by Dick Wolf as the third installment of the Chicago franchise. It stars Nick Gehlfuss, Yaya DaCosta, Torrey DeVitto, Rachel DiPillo, Colin Donnell, Brian Tee, S. Epatha Merkerson, Oliver Platt, Marlyne Barrett, Norma Kuhling, Dominic Rains, Steven Weber, Guy Lockard, Kristen Hager, Jessy Schram, Luke Mitchell, Sarah Ramos and Darren Barnet. It premiered on November 17, 2015. Chicago Med follows the emergency department doctors and nurses of the fictional Gaffney Chicago Medical Center. As they work to save lives, these members deal with the personal and professional pressures of their jobs.

8. House

An American medical drama television series created by David Shore that originally aired on Fox from November 16, 2004, to May 21, 2012 for eight seasons. It features the life of Dr. Gregory House (Hugh Laurie), an unconventional, misanthropic, cynical medical genius who, despite his dependence on pain medication, successfully leads a team of diagnosticians at the fictional Princeton–Plainsboro Teaching Hospital (PPTH) in New Jersey. House often clashes with his fellow physicians, including his own diagnostic team, because many of his hypotheses about patients' illnesses are based on subtle or controversial insights, and his flouting of hospital rules and procedures frequently leads him into conflict with his boss, hospital administrator and Dean of Medicine Dr. Lisa Cuddy (Lisa Edelstein). House's only true friend is Dr. James Wilson (Robert Sean Leonard), head of the Department of Oncology.

Oz is an American prison drama television series created, co-executive produced, and principally written by Tom Fontana.[1][2] Set at a fictional men's prison, it was the first one-hour dramatic television series produced by the premium cable network HBO.[3] Oz ran for six seasons, from its premiere on July 12, 1997, to its series finale on February 23, 2003.

Hosted by actor LeVar Burton, the show encouraged a love of reading by featuring children’s books read aloud, often with animations or celebrity narrators. Each episode focused on a theme—such as animals, friendship, or history—with Burton introducing the topic, interviewing real-world professionals, and connecting the stories to everyday life.
